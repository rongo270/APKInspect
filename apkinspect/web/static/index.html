The book of APKInspect

APKInspect opens an Android app package (.apk or .aab), reads its manifest and every bundled file, and reports the security problems it finds — then scores the app from 100 (safe) down to 0 (fully exposed).

What it inspects

🔑
Secrets & API keys

Private keys, cloud credentials, Google/Stripe/Slack keys and high-entropy passwords hard-coded in the build.

🚪
Exported components

Activities, services, receivers and providers that any other app can reach — including cleartext http:// deep links.

🌐
Exposed backends

Firebase databases and storage buckets whose URLs ship in the app and may have open rules.

⚙️
Build configuration

Debuggable builds, allowed backups, cleartext traffic and other risky manifest flags.

🛡️
Permissions

Privacy-sensitive and powerful permissions, each rated by how much they widen the attack surface.

📦
APK & AAB

Reads compiled binary-XML manifests (APK) and protobuf manifests (AAB) with zero external tools.

How the score works

Every app starts at 100. Each issue chips away at the score by an amount based on its severity, with diminishing returns for repeats so a long tail of minor issues can't unfairly zero a sound app.

How to use it

  1. Go to the Scanner tab and drop in an .apk or .aab.
  2. Read the score and the findings, grouped by severity.
  3. Expand any finding and click “How to block this →” to jump here for the full explanation and fix.

Note: this is static analysis — it flags the presence of risks, not proven exploitability. Always confirm a finding before acting on it.
